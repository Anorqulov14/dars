Uzbekistan

Uzbekistan ( Uzbek: Oʻzbekiston / Ўзбетисто [ozbekiˈstɒn] ), officially the Republic of Uzbekistan ( Uzbek: Oʻzbekiston Respublikasi / Ўзбетисто Respublikasi ) [ note 1 ] is a country located in the central part of Central Asia . It is a neutral state [ 7 ] . The area is 448,924 km². The population, according to the State Statistics Committee as of April 22, 2024, exceeded 37,000,000 people. Adjacent states: to the east - Kyrgyzstan ; to the northeast, north and northwest - Kazakhstan ; to the southwest and south - Turkmenistan ; to the south - Afghanistan , and to the southeast - Tajikistan . It has access to the almost dried up Aral Sea , but is one of two countries in the world (along with Liechtenstein ) that need to cross the territory of at least two states to access the World Ocean , since all countries adjacent to Uzbekistan also do not have access to the World Ocean [ 8 ] . The capital is Tashkent . The official language throughout the country is Uzbek , in the Republic of Karakalpakstan (along with Uzbek) its own official language has been established - Karakalpak . Uzbekistan was formed in 1924 as the Uzbek SSR - a republic within the USSR , since 1991 - the independent Republic of Uzbekistan.
